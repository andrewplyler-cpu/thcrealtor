App State Student Housing & Investment Properties

Appalachian State University drives Boone's largest and most consistent rental demand. As a Boone native and App State alumnus, this is a market I know from the inside.

Why App State Housing Is a Different Market

Student housing near App State has different fundamentals than typical vacation rentals or primary home sales. Lease cycles align with the academic year. Demand is predictable year after year, driven by enrollment growth that Appalachian State has sustained for decades. And proximity to campus — walking distance, bus route access, and minutes to King Street — determines value in ways that don't apply elsewhere.

The things that matter here:

  • AppalCART bus route access. App State students rely on the university's 13 fare-free transit routes. Properties on or near major routes command stronger rental demand and lower vacancy.
  • Walking distance to campus. Properties within 10–15 minutes' walk of campus core command premium rents.
  • Bedroom count over square footage. In student housing, 4 bedrooms at $600/room outperforms a larger 2-bedroom at the same price. Think per-bedroom, not per-unit.
  • Town of Boone zoning. Boone's zoning map has 21 district codes (last amended December 4, 2023). Knowing a property's zoning before you buy is non-negotiable.

For Families of App State Students

If your student is heading to Boone and you're considering buying rather than paying rent for four years, the math often works in your favor.

A typical scenario: parents purchase a 3- or 4-bedroom home or condo near campus. Their student lives there; the other bedrooms rent to classmates at market rate. The rental income offsets the mortgage significantly, and when the student graduates, the parents either sell (typically at a gain) or continue holding it as an investment property.

I've walked multiple App State families through exactly this process. What I'll help you evaluate:

  • Purchase price vs. total rental income potential
  • Which neighborhoods and property types work best for this strategy
  • Town of Boone zoning compliance for multi-tenant use
  • AppalCART route access for each property
  • Realistic rental rates based on current comparable leases

For Investors

The App State market doesn't have the seasonal volatility of pure vacation rental markets. Demand is consistent, the tenant pool is predictable, and Boone's continued enrollment growth provides a long-term tailwind. Investment property types that perform well:

  • Single-family homes with 3–5 bedrooms in walkable neighborhoods
  • Condos and townhomes near the King Street corridor
  • Small multi-unit properties (duplexes, triplexes) in eligible zoning districts
  • Properties with multiple bedrooms and proximity to bus routes

I provide honest investment analysis — not inflated projections. If a property doesn't make sense as an App State rental, I'll tell you before you buy it.
